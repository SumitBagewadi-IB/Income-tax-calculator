Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between Old and New Tax Regime?

The Old Regime allows various deductions (80C, 80D, HRA, etc.) but has higher base rates. The New Regime offers lower slab rates but most deductions are not available. From FY 2023-24, the New Regime is the default.

What is the standard deduction for salaried employees?

For FY 2025-26, the standard deduction is ₹75,000 under the New Regime and ₹50,000 under the Old Regime for salaried employees and pensioners.

Is there a rebate under Section 87A?

Yes. Under the New Regime, a full tax rebate is available if your net taxable income does not exceed ₹7,00,000. Under the Old Regime, the rebate applies up to ₹5,00,000.

What is Health and Education Cess?

A 4% cess is levied on the income tax amount (including surcharge, if applicable) for all taxpayers.

What is surcharge and when does it apply?

Surcharge applies when income exceeds ₹50 lakhs: 10% for ₹50L–₹1Cr, 15% for ₹1Cr–₹2Cr, 25% for ₹2Cr–₹5Cr, and 37% (Old) / 25% (New) above ₹5Cr.

Can I switch between Old and New Regime every year?

Salaried individuals without business income can switch between regimes every year. Taxpayers with business income can switch only once.
